Here is more about the camel

      - Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
      - Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
      - Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

Here is more about the chameleon

      - There are around 160 species of chameleon.
      - Almost half of the world's chameleon species are native to Madagascar, they are also found in Africa, and southern Europe to as far as Sri Lanka
      - Special color pigment cells under the skin called chromatophores allow some chameleon species to change their skin color, creating combined patterns

Here is more about the Panda

      - These magnificent mammals are omnivores. But whilst pandas will occasionally eat small animals and fish, bamboo counts for 99 percent of their diet.
      - Pandas are BIG eaters – every day they fill their tummies for up to 12 hours, shifting up to 12 kilograms of bamboo!
      - The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.

Here is more about the teddy bear

      - The word or name ‘teddy bear’ comes from the nickname of Theodore Roosevelt.
      - Before teddy bears could be sold, Michtom had to get President Roosevelt’s permission to use his name
      - Older teddy bears, that were created in the early 20th century, actually looked like real bears!

Here is more about the cicada

      - There are around 3,000 cicada species, according to National Geographic
      - The buzzing sound they make is a mating call.
      - Cicadas are found only in the eastern half of the United States.
